Bill Russel

About Bill

Bill Russell was a legendary American basketball player and coach, celebrated for leading the Boston Celtics to 11 NBA championships in 13 seasons between 1956 and 1969. Born in Louisiana, he became the NBA's first Black coach of a major professional team in 1966 and was a dedicated civil rights activist who used his platform to advocate for social justice.
