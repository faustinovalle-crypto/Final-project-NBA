About Lebron

LeBron James is an American professional basketball player born in Akron, Ohio, on December 30, 1984. Known as "King James," he is widely considered one of the greatest players of all time, becoming the NBA's all-time leading scorer. He has won four NBA championships with three different teams (the Cavaliers, Heat, and Lakers) and has won multiple MVP awards, Olympic gold medals, and numerous other accolades. James was a high school phenom, leading St. Vincent-St. Mary High School to three state championships before being drafted first overall by the Cleveland Cavaliers in 2003.
